## Captain
:
A half-elf, adorned in the ribbons and medals of a
high-ranking commander, sits astride an armored
warhorse. To her right, a powerfully built orcish man
in more pedestrian attire sits atop his own horse,
shouldering a hefty greataxe. They set down the
road together, accompanied by blaring trumpets and
banging drums, which announce their approach.
Puffing on a pipe and eyeing the room, a halfling
guildmaster in a fitted suit twirls a dagger between
his fingers. A crowd of thieves, crooks, and bandits
are arrayed before him, all studying blueprints laid
out on a candlelit table. Tonight, their chapter of the
Thieves’ Guild becomes rich, and their guildmaster
goes down in history.
Kobolds swarm left and right, scampering across
piles of gold pieces, only to be met by the dwarf ’s
greataxe. Arrow after arrow, a human archer looses
shots that merely plink off the enraged dragon’s
crimson scales. A gnomish mage gathers crackling
arcane might in his hands for a devastating magical
blast. Across the chamber, amidst the chaos, a plan
crystallizes in the adventuring company’s leader.
She begins shouting orders, and the party sets into
motion.
Decisive leaders and brilliant strategists, captains
inspire their allies to greatness. Invariably, they stand
alongside their cohorts—their devoted lieutenants,

whom they entrust with their lives. Captains out-
strategize their enemies, ensuring that, while they rarely deal the killing blow, they’re always on the winning side.

### Fearless Leaders
Many people aspire to command, but few inspire
loyalty as a captain does. Captains rally allies to
their side through confidence and determination,
but gain long-term friends through wisdom and
accountability.
A good captain thinks a little bigger than their
compatriots, planning one step further and fitting
their allies like puzzle pieces to the challenges ahead.
To their eyes, there are few defeats; only setbacks,
diversions, and detours. Success is always on the path
ahead.

Moreover, captains lead by example. They stand
shoulder-to-shoulder with knights, creep through
the night alongside rogues, and study arcane texts
with mages, even when they barely understand what
they’re reading. Captains are at the front line with
their allies, not barking orders from afar.
### Cohort and Commander
A captain is only as good as their second in
command, their cohort, who stands by them at every
step and safeguards their plan’s success. Far more
than a simple ally, a captain’s cohort is a confidant,
an advisor, and a trusted friend. Cohorts see their
captain as a wellspring of information, and will
follow them to the ends of the earth.

\column

### Creating a Captain
The ideal captain—a consummate leader—can be
realized in many different ways. Your captain could
be a head of an adventuring company, an officer in
an army, the president of a corporation, the chief of
a tribe, the admiral of a fleet, or any other type of
leader. The only commonalities amongst captains are
their positions of authority and natural inclinations
towards leadership.
You should consider how your captain chooses
to lead: not simply what banner they fly, but how
they treat their followers and how they give orders.
Do you rally others to your side with a magnetic
personality or a stoic facade? How would you deal
with a dilemma that offers only losing strategies?
How do you treat your opponents once you’ve
secured victory?
You should also think of previous victories or
defeats that have defined your captain in the past.
Has your story been that of a meteoric rise or a
disgraceful fall? What event first thrust you into
leadership, and how did you first take to it? Which
success or defeat led you to the life of an adventurer?

#### Quick build
You can make a captain quickly by following these
suggestions. First, make Charisma your highest
ability score, followed by Strength. Next, select
History and Persuasion as your skills, and take the
Dueling Fighting Style.

## Class Features

As a captain, you gain the following class features.

#### Hit Points
**Hit Dice:**                    :: 1d8 per captain level
**Hit Points at 1st Level:**     :: 8 + your Constitution modifier
**Hit Points at Higher Levels:** :: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per captain level after 1st

#### Proficiencies
**Armor:**   :: Light armor, Medium armor, Heavy armor, Shields
**Weapons:** :: Simple weapons, martial weapons
**Tools:**   :: None

**Saving Throws:** :: Charisma, Constitution
**Skills:**        :: Choose two from Animal Handling, Athletics, Deception, History, Insight, Intimidation, Perception, and Persuasion

#### Equipment
You start with the following equipment, in addition to
the equipment granted to you by your background:
- *(a)* scale mail, or *(b)* leather armor
- A dagger and *(a)* a warhammer, *(b)* a longsword, or *(c)* any simple weapon
- *(a)* a light crossbow and 20 bolts, *(b)* a shortbow and 20 arrows, or *(c)* 5 javelins

### Born Leader
By 1st level, your competence and commanding personality lend you a knack for leadership. Common folk defer to your authority and influence, especially in times of crisis. As a result, you have advantage on any Charisma (Persuasion) check.

Additionally, you can use your Charisma modifier, instead of your Intelligence modifier, for any ability check you make related to planning, strategy, or tactics.

### Fighting Style
At 1st level, you adopt a style of fighting as your
specialty. Choose one of the following options. You
can’t take a Fighting Style option more than once,
even if you later get to choose again.
#### Archery
You gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls you make with ranged weapons.
#### Defense
While you are wearing armor, you gain a +1 bonus to AC.
#### Dueling
When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapons, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls with that weapon.

#### Protection
When a creature you can see attacks a target other than you that is within 5 feet of you, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll. You must be wielding a shield.

### Cohort
Starting at 2nd level, you gain a loyal cohort who carries your banner and follows your lead.
#### Initiating a Cohort
Select a companion from the Cohort section; following an 8-hour initiation period, that creature becomes your cohort. You can have only one cohort at a time.

If your cohort is ever slain, you can raise them from the dead by the usual methods, such as the raise dead spell, or you can spend 8 hours initiating a new cohort. If you do so, your previous cohort loses all abilities granted by this ability, even if it is later returned to life.

Your cohort obeys your commands as best it can. Your cohort takes its turn immediately before or after your turn each round (your choice), and you determine its actions, decisions, attitudes, and so on.

If you are incapacitated or absent, your companion acts on its own.
#### Cohort Features
At 2nd, 6th, 10th, 14th, and 18th levels, your cohort gains new abilities based on its archetype.
\column
#### Cohort Save DC
If your Cohort forces a creature to make a saving throw, the DC is 8 plus your Charisma modifier and your Proficiency bonus. 
#### Cohort Spell Attack Bonus
If your Cohort makes a spell attack, it uses your Cohort Spell Attack Bonus, which is equal to your Charisma modifier + your Proficiency Bonus.


#### Hit Points
For each captain level you gain after 2nd, your cohort gains an additional hit die and increases its hit points accordingly.

#### Ability Score
Whenever you gain the Ability Score Improvement class feature, your cohort’s abilities also improve. Your cohort can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or it can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, your companion can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature, unless its description specifies otherwise.

When you improve a cohort’s ability scores, remember to also increase other relevant statistics, such as skill bonuses, bonuses to hit and damage, and spell save DCs.

#### Alignment
Your cohort’s alignment is within one step of your own, and has a personality trait and flaw determined by the GM. Its bond is always, “I will follow my commander into any fray, and I will gladly fight by their side until my death.”
#### Proficiencies
Your Cohort has proficiency in skills and saving throws listed in its statistics. Your Cohort uses your Proficiency Bonus instead of its own. Because Cohort statistics already include a +2 Proficiency Bonus, you need only adjust skill and saving throw proficiencies starting at 5th level.

### Banner
Starting at 3rd level, you adopt a banner which you fly in combat, emblazon on your armor, and fix to your seal. You can select from the banners which are detailed at the end of the class description. Your choice grants you features at 3rd level, and again at 7th, 13th, and 17th level.

### War Tactics
Starting at 3rd level, you have a pool of battle dice that you can use to bolster your allies or perform special maneuvers in combat. You begin with 2 battle dice, which are d8s. You regain all expended battle dice when you finish a short or long rest, or when you roll initiative.

Your battle die changes and more battle dice become available when you reach certain levels in this class, as shown on the Battle Dice column of the Captain table.

#### Saving Throws
If one of your maneuvers calls for a saving throw to resist its effects, the saving throw DC is calculated as follows:

**Maneuver save DC** = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength or Dexterity modifier

#### Maneuvers
You can spend battle dice to use the following maneuvers:

**Brace.** When an ally you can see within 60 feet of yourself fails a saving throw, you can take a Reaction to expend one Battle Die and add it to the roll, potentially turning it into a success.

**Rally.** As a bonus action on your turn, you can expend a battle die to choose one allied creature within 60 feet of you who can see or hear you. That creature regains hit points equal to the number rolled + your Charisma modifier. You can't use this ability to heal a creature who has 0 hit points.

**Staggering Strike.** As a bonus action when you make a weapon attack against a creature, you can expend a battle die to attempt to stun a humanoid target. On a hit, the target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or be incapacitated until the start of your next turn.

**Bolster.**
As a Bonus Action, you can expend one Battle Die to
motivate an ally within 60 feet of yourself. The next time
your ally makes an attack before the start of your next
turn, it adds the Battle Die to the attack and damage roll.


### Ability Score Improvement
When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

\column

### Combo Action
Starting at 5th level, when a creature you or your cohort can see makes an attack, each of you can use your reaction to enhance that attack with a Combo Action (detailed below).

A creature who assists in this way is referred to as a "collaborator." If there are multiple collaborators for a single Combo Action, each collaborator takes their reaction in initiative order.

After all collaborators have used their reactions, the original attacker makes the attack roll to complete the Combo Action.

If a Combo Action requires a saving throw to resist its effects, the DC for the saving throw is **Maneuver save DC**

### Valiant Surge
By 7th level, whenever you or your Cohort score a Critical Hit or reduce an enemy to 0 Hit Points, you regain an expended Battle Die
### Advanced Tactics
At 9th level, when you use a maneuver that targets an ally, you can target a second ally within range without expending an additional Battle Die.

### Coordinated Strike
By 11th level, you and your cohort fight as a perfect fighting unit. Whenever you attack a creature that your cohort hit with an attack since the end of your last turn, you deal an additional 2d8 damage on a hit.

### Lead by Example
Starting at 15th level, whenever you roll a 20 on an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, each friendly creature within 30 feet of you has advantage when it makes the same type of roll (attack roll, ability check, or saving throw) before the beginning of your next turn.

### Legendary Commander
At 20th level, immediately after another creature’s turn, you can choose yourself or an ally within 60 feet of yourself that can see or hear you to take a Legendary Action. This action is one of the following: Attack (If a creature takes the Attack action as a legendary action, it can attack more than once if it has a feature, like Extra Attack, that gives it extra attacks.), Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Hide, Cast a Spell, or Use a Object.

A creature can only take one Legendary Action each round.

When a creature takes the Dash or Disengage action in this way, the benefits of that action last until the end of the creature’s next turn.

You can use this feature to take three Legendary Actions and regain all expended uses when you finish a Short or Long Rest.

## Combo actions 
Each Combo action has a prerequisite, usually dictating the maximum distance a collaborator can be from the attack's target to assist. For ease of use, Combo actions are divided into Melee, Ranged, and Spellcasting options.

### Melee Combos
When you use your reaction to collaborate on a combo
attack, you can choose one of the following Combo actions:

#### Ally Launcher
*Prerequisite: The attacker is within your reach and within 5 feet of the target, your carrying capacity is greater than the attack’s weight, and the target is no more than one size larger than you.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You toss the attacker through
the air, over the enemy, allowing them to strike from above
and land in a different position.
:
***Combo Effect.*** Your unorthodox assault opens up a weakness in the enemy’s guard. The attacker can roll an additional d20 as a part of their attack roll, choosing between it or their own roll.

In addition, the attacker lands in an unoccupied space of their choice within 5 feet of the target.

#### Arcane assault
*Prerequisite: The attacker is within the reach of a cantrip you know which makes melee spell attack.*::

**Collaborator’s Action.** If the attack's target is within your cantrip's reach you cast the cantrip against the target striking in unison with your ally.

On a hit, you deal only one damage die, and you don’t add your ability modifier to the damage if you could.
:
**Combo Effect.** Whether you hit or miss, or if the target is not within your reach, your attack imbues your ally with arcane power. The attacker can choose to make their attack deal damage of your cantrip's type instead of the normal damage type. 

#### Double Grapple
*Prerequisite: The attacker and the target are within your reach.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You move in sync with your ally to restrain the target together. Make a Strength (*Athletics*) check contested by the target’s Strength (*Athletics*) or Dexterity (*Acrobatics*) check. If you succeed, the target is grappled by both you and your ally. A target two or more sizes larger than you automatically succeeds in this contest.
:
***Combo Effect.*** While the target is grappled by collaborators, it has disadvantage on attack rolls and Dexterity saving throws.
Additionally, the target’s movement speed is reduced to 0, and it cannot escape the grapple unless it breaks free from grapplers.


#### Dazing Strike
*Prerequisite: The attacker and the target are within your reach.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You deliver a quick, forceful blow aimed at the target’s head or a vulnerable spot to throw it off balance. Make a melee weapon attack. On a hit, you deal damage as usual, but you don’t add your ability modifier to the damage unless it is negative.
:
***Combo Effect.*** Whether you hit or miss, the target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or become dazed until the end of the attacker’s next turn. While dazed, the target can’t take reactions.

#### Goading Rush
*Prerequisite: You are within 15 feet of the attack's target.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You charge into the fray, tearing
the target’s attention away from your ally and onto yourself.

You move towards the target of the attack, ending your
movement as close to them as possible as you distract them
with a goading insult.
:
***Combo Effect.*** The target has disadvantage on attack
rolls against creatures other than you until the end of the
attacker’s next turn.


#### Staggering Charge
*Prerequisite: The attacker and their target are within your reach.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You barrel into the target, attempting to knock it off balance as your ally strikes. Make a Strength (*Athletics*) check contested by the target’s Strength (*Athletics*) or Dexterity (*Acrobatics*) check. If you win the contest, you knock the target prone or shove it 5 feet away from you (your choice). A target two or more sizes larger than you automatically succeeds in this contest.
:
***Combo Effect.*** Whether you win the contest or not,
your charge serves as a distraction. The attacker can roll an
additional d20 as part of their attack roll, choosing between
it or their own roll.


#### Unison Onslaught
*Prerequisite: The attack's target is within your reach.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You make a swift melee weapon
attack against the target, striking in unison with your ally.

On a hit, you deal damage as normal, but you don’t add
your ability modifier to the damage of the attack, unless
that modifier is negative.
:
***Combo Effect.*** Whether you hit or miss, your attack serves as a potent distraction. The attacker can roll an additional d20 as a part of their attack roll, choosing between it or their own roll.

### Ranged Combos
When you use your reaction to collaborate on a combo attack, you can choose one of the following Combo actions:


#### Blinding Volley
*Prerequisite: The target is within range of a ranged or thrown weapon you are wielding.*

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You fire a shot aimed at the target’s eyes or other sensitive area, attempting to momentarily impair their vision. Make a ranged weapon attack. On a hit, you deal your weapon’s damage as usual, but don’t add your ability modifier to the damage, unless that modifier is negative.

***Combo Effect.*** Whether you hit or miss, your shot forces the target to make a Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, the target has disadvantage on the next attack roll it makes before the end of the attacker’s next turn.

#### Disarming Combination
*Prerequisite: The attack’s target is within range of a ranged or thrown weapon you are wielding.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You attempt to strike an object the target is holding with a ranged attack, potentially knocking it loose from its grasp.
:
***Combo Effect.*** The target makes a Strength save with disadvantage. On a failure, it drops one item it’s holding (your choice).

#### Flinchshot
*Prerequisite: The attack’s target is within range of a ranged or thrown weapon you are wielding.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You make a swift ranged weapon
attack, perfectly timed to momentarily distract an enemy.

On a hit, you deal your weapon’s damage as normal, but you don’t add your ability modifier to the damage of the attack, unless that modifier is negative.
:
***Combo Effect.*** Hit or miss, your attack serves as a potent
distraction. The attacker can roll an additional d20 as part
of their attack roll, choosing between it or their own roll.




#### Pinning Shot
*Prerequisite: The target is within range of a ranged or thrown weapon you are wielding.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You take careful aim at a vulnerable spot, attempting to pin or impair the target’s movement. Make a ranged weapon attack against the target. On a hit, you deal damage as usual, but you don’t add your ability modifier to the damage, unless that modifier is negative.
:
***Combo Effect.*** Hit or miss, the target’s movement speed is reduced by 10 feet until the end of the attacker’s next turn. If the attacker’s attack also hits, the target’s speed is further reduced by an additional 10 feet.

#### Marking Flare
*Prerequisite: The attack’s target is within range of a ranged or thrown weapon you are wielding.*::

***Collaborator's Action.*** You fire a specially-prepared piece of ammunition at the target. Make a ranged weapon attack. On a hit, you deal no damage, but the target is marked with a phosphorescent, alchemical substance.
:
***Combo Effect.*** The attacker's attack roll ignores disadvantage from the target being lightly obscured (such as in dim light or fog). If your collaborative attack hit the target, the mark flares brightly, and the next attack roll made against the target by an ally other than you or the attacker has advantage. The mark then fades.
#### Suppressing Fire
*Prerequisite: The attack’s target is within range of a ranged or thrown weapon you are wielding.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You provide a well-timed ranged
attack, targeting the space between the attacker and their
target, which provides cover for your ally to strike and retreat.
:
***Combo Effect.*** The attacker can roll an additional d20
as a part of their attack roll, choosing between it or their
own roll. In addition, until the end of the instigator’s turn,
they don’t provoke opportunity attacks from the target.



### Spellcasting Combos
A spellcaster can collaborate in a combo attack while they
are within 90 feet of the attacker by choosing one spell the
spellcaster can cast and expending a spell slot of any level.

The effect of the combo attack depends on the school of magic the chosen spell belongs to.

#### Abjuration

*Prerequisite: You are within 90 feet of the attacker, have an unexpended spell slot, and know or have prepared an abjuration spell of 1st level or higher.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You expend one spell slot.
Sparkling magic shimmers around the attacker’s body, fortifying them with temporary, spectral armour.
:
***Combo Effect.*** The next time the attacker takes damage
within the next minute, that damage is reduced by an amount equal to five times the level of the spell slot expended, and the armour disappears.
{{pageNumber,auto}}
\page
#### Conjuration

*Prerequisite: You are within 90 feet of the attacker, have an unexpended spell slot, and know or have prepared a conjuration spell of 1st level or higher.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You expend one spell slot. Your
magic spirals around the attacker, wrenching them from harm’s way after they make their attack.
:
***Combo Effect.*** Immediately after the attacker makes their attack against the target, they can choose to teleport to an unoccupied space that they can see within a number of feet equal to 10 x the level of the spell slot expended.





#### Divination
*Prerequisite: You are within 90 feet of the attacker, have an unexpended spell slot, and know or have prepared a divination spell of 1st level or higher.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You expend one spell slot. Your magic enhances the senses of the attacker with supernatural precognition.
:
***Combo Effect.*** The attacker gains a bonus to all saving throws equal to the level of the spell slot expended until the start of their next turn.

#### Enchantment

*Prerequisite: You are within 90 feet of the attacker, have an unexpended spell slot, and know or have prepared an enchantment spell of 1st level or higher.*::

***Collaborator’s Action.*** You expend one spell slot. Hypnotic magic weaves through the force of the attacker’s attack to strike the target’s mind.
:

## Banners
Captains adopt emblems that denote their virtues, skills, and command styles, which they fly upon their banners to rally allies to their side. A symbolic statement, this heraldry speaks volumes about what a captain prizes in the world, be it knowledge, allies, or personal gain.

### Astra Militarum Banner

Not all glory is won through heroism alone. Captains who adopt the Astra Militarum Banner are products of the Imperium's most vast and unbreakable force: the disciplined legions of the Imperial Guard.
 They are not lone champions; they are masters of cohesion, precision, and overwhelming force, where success is measured not by individual valor, but by the survival of the regiment.
Trained to think in formations, supply lines, and synchronized volleys, these Captains lead their forces like extensions of their own will.

 Every shot, every charge, every coordinated strike is a testament to countless drills, iron discipline, and the unshakable belief that victory is the product of collective action.
 
#### Maneuver: Tactical Charge
When you reach 3rd Level, you learn to position yourself and your allies to maximize your collaborative potential. As a Bonus Action, you can expend a Battle Die to move yourself or your cohort up to half your speed without provoking opportunity attacks. 

If this movement ends with you and your cohort within 5 feet of the same enemy, the next Attack or Combo Action performed against that enemy before the start of your next turn deals extra damage equal to the Battle Die rolled.

#### Imperial Combo
At 6th level, when you or your cohort uses a melee or ranged Combo Action, you add your Charisma modifier to the damage roll.

Furthermore, the target of a successful Combo Action has disadvantage on the next saving throw it makes against a Captain maneuver or cohort ability before the end of your next turn.

#### Master of Tactics
As you reach 10th level, your mastery of battlefield drills allows you to execute any maneuver at a moment's notice. You or your cohort can use any Melee or Ranged Combo Action without needing to meet its specific prerequisites.

#### Synchronized Assault
At 14th level, your rigorous command drills elevate your unit’s coordination to lethal precision. When you or your cohort use a Combo Action, you can choose one friendly creature within 30 feet who can see or hear you. 

That creature can immediately use its reaction to make one weapon attack against the same target of the Combo Action, provided it is within range.

\column

#### Coordinated Annihilation
At 18th level, you can execute a devastating finishing move. As an Action, you can command your cohort to move up to its speed toward a target and perform a Combo Action with you as the collaborator, regardless of whether you have already used your reactions. This special Combo Action does not require a reaction from either of you.

On a hit, the attack deals an additional 5d10 damage of the weapon's type, and the target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw against your Maneuver save DC or be Stunned until the end of your next turn. 

Once you use this feature, you cannot do so again until you finish a Long Rest.

### Beast Banner
Some Captains lead armies, others command fleets, but those who bear the Beast Banner fight side by side with a trusted companion. This primal bond transcends words, a partnership forged in battle and loyalty.

#### Primal Cohort

At 3rd level, you gain a loyal beast companion, known as your Cohort. This creature is a fierce and untamed warrior that fights alongside you. You choose its appearance, but its statistics are determined by the Primal Beast statblock.

Your Cohort obeys your commands in combat, taking its turn immediately after yours.

If you are incapacitated, your Primal Beast can act on its own.

If your Primal Beast has died,  you can perform a ritual over 8 hours to return it to life with its maximum hit points.


#### Maneuver:Bonded Tactics

Also at 3rd level, you and your beast fight as one. When you take the Attack action and expend a battle die, if your Cohort is within 5 feet of a target you are attacking, you gain advantage on the first attack roll against that creature on your turn.

Additionally, when your Primal Beast hits a creature with an attack, you can make one weapon attack against that creature.

#### Pack Coordination

At 6th level, your tactical mastery extends to your Primal Beast. When you command your Primal Beast to take the Attack action, it can make one additional attack as part of that action.

Additionally, whenever you and your cohort are collaborators of the same combo action, neither of you expend your reaction to do it.

#### Ferocious Assault

At 10th level, your Cohort’s strikes become deadlier. Your Primal Beast’s attacks count as magical for the purposes of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks.

Additionally, when you hit a creature with an attack on your turn, your Primal Beast can immediately use its reaction to make one melee attack against the same target.

#### Shared Instincts
At 14th level, the bond between you and your Primal Beast deepens, allowing you to move and strike in perfect harmony. When you move on your turn, your Primal Beast can move up to half its speed as part of your movement, without using its own movement. 

In addition, the first time either you or your Primal Beast scores a critical hit on your turn, the other gains advantage on its next attack roll made before the end of that turn.


\column

#### Apex Predator

At 18th level, your bond with your beast reaches its peak, turning the two of you into an unstoppable force. When you and your Primal Beast are both within 10 feet of a hostile creature, that creature has disadvantage on attack rolls against both of you.

Additionally, when your Primal Beast takes damage that would reduce it to 0 hit points, it can make a Constitution saving throw (DC 10 + damage taken). 

On a success, it drops to 1 hit point instead.

Captains who bear the Dagger Banner—an insignia such as a tattoo or scar shaped like a blade—excel in stealth and cunning tactics to overcome their enemies. At 3rd level, your experience in striking from the shadows grants you proficiency with the Sleight of Hand and Stealth skills if you don’t already have them.

You also learn two cantrips of your choice from the cleric spell list. These cantrips are considered Captain spells for you, and Charisma is your spellcasting ability when casting them.

Additionally, you can now use Rally to restore hit points to a creature that has 0 hit points, allowing you to revive the dying through sheer will.

At 6th level, your unwavering conviction inspires those who fight under your command. When you use your Blitz feature to command an ally affected by the Charmed or Frightened condition, that creature can immediately reroll its saving throw against that effect. On a success, the condition ends.

battle die. You can arrange these cubes as you wish,
but each cube must have at least one face adjacent
to the face of another cube. This area is magically
filled with natural terrain chosen by the GM, such as
boulders, fallen trees, or gusting winds. You choose
whether the area is difficult terrain or completely
impassable. Each cube of terrain has AC 15, 15 HP,
and immunity to poison and psychic damage.
Once you use this maneuver, you can’t use it again for
1 minute.

#### Druidbane
By 6th level, you have gotten wise to Feywild’s terrainmanipulating tricks. You and your cohort ignore
magical and nonmagical difficult terrain. Additionally,
you are immune to the spell spike growth and other
spells that affect the natural terrain, such as the spells
hallucinatory terrain and plant growth.

#### Rooted Resilience
At 10th level, your bond with the natural world grants you and your Cohort extraordinary fortitude. When you or your Cohort start your turn standing on natural terrain or difficult terrain created by your Blocked Passage maneuver or Thornfoot feature, you gain temporary hit points equal to your Captain level. 

Additionally, while standing on such terrain, you and your Cohort have advantage on saving throws against being knocked prone or moved against your will.

#### Superior Positioning
At 14th level, your ability to use the land to your
advantage is unparalleled. You and each friendly
creature within 15 feet of you have advantage on
attack rolls against creatures that are standing in
difficult terrain.

wide area of thorns behind you, as per the spell spike

These thorns
last for 10 minutes, or until you choose to dismiss

### Tarantula Banner

Captains who fly the Tarantula Banner are patient, deadly hunters who use stealth, poison, and entangling webs to isolate and eliminate their foes. They are masters of ambush, climbing to unseen vantage points and waiting for the perfect moment to strike. Whether they lead a cell of assassins or a quiet network of spies, these captains treat every conflict as a hunt, and they are the apex predators.

#### Spider's Climb
Starting when you choose this banner at 3rd level, your physical prowess adapts to that of an arachnid. You gain a climbing speed equal to your walking speed, and you can climb difficult surfaces, including upside down on ceilings, without needing to make an ability check.

#### Maneuver: Ensnaring Strike
Also at 3rd level, you learn a new maneuver.
When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one Battle Die as a bonus action to shoot sticky, entangling webs at the target. The target must succeed on a Strength saving throw against your Maneuver save DC or be Restrained until the end of your next turn.

#### Poisoner's Art
At 6th level, you have mastered the use of venom. You gain proficiency with the poisoner's kit, and both you and your Cohort gain resistance to poison damage.

Additionally, you have advantage on attack rolls against any creature that is Surprised, Restrained, or Incapacitated.

#### Web Sense
At 10th level, your senses sharpen, attuning you to the slightest vibrations. You gain tremorsense out to a range of 10 feet.

If you are in contact with a web, this tremorsense expands to a 30-foot radius.

#### Maneuver: Paralyzing Strike
At 14th level, you learn the following maneuver:
When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend two Battle Dice as a bonus action to inject a potent paralyzing venom. 

The target must make a Constitution saving throw against your Maneuver save DC. 

On a failed save, the target takes additional poison damage equal to the total rolled on both Battle Dice and is Paralyzed until the end of your next turn. On a successful save, the target takes half as much damage and is not Paralyzed.

\column

#### Spider's Lair
Starting at 18th level, you can claim a battlefield as your own hunting ground. As an action, you can expend three Battle Dice to instantly create a 30-foot radius of thick, magical webs centered on yourself. This area becomes difficult terrain, and the webs are lightly obscured. 

The webs last for 1 minute or until you dismiss them (no action required).

Any creature (other than you and your Cohort) that starts its turn in the webs or enters the area for the first time on a turn must make a Strength saving throw against your Maneuver save DC. On a failed save, the creature is Restrained. A creature can use its action to make a Strength check against your Maneuver save DC, freeing itself on a success.

While you are within this webbed area, you gain the benefits of your Web Sense at all times, and you can use a bonus action on your turn to pull one creature Restrained by these webs 15 feet closer to you.

### Yellow Sign Banner
The Yellow Sign is a twisting symbol of eldritch chaos, the
signature of an otherworldly leviathan ready to consume
all of reality. Captains bearing its distinctive mark upon
their Shields and armor aren’t commanders—they are
cult leaders dedicated to incomprehensible alien masters.
Their objectives are as secretive as they are disquieting,
but undoubtedly demand indoctrination of more thralls
to spread their master’s sign and influence.

#### Maneuver: Frenzied Strike
Starting at 3rd level, when you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack or an unarmed strike, you can expend one Battle Die as a bonus action to infect the target’s mind with frenzy. The creature gains the Frenzied condition until the end of its next turn.

A creature affected by the Frenzied condition cannot distinguish between friend and foe, regarding all creatures it can see as enemies. While Frenzied, it must choose targets for its attacks, spells, and abilities randomly from the creatures it can see within range. Additionally, it must make opportunity attacks against any creatures that provoke them.

#### Eldritch Herald
Also at 3rd level, your eldritch master guides your path, granting you the following benefits. When you finish a long rest, you gain proficiency and expertise in a skill chosen by your DM. 

The DM may change this skill each time you complete a long rest. 

Furthermore, you and your Cohort have advantage on saving throws to avoid or end the Charmed and Frightened conditions.

#### Pallid Aura
At 6th level, you learn to trace the Yellow Sign in the air, creating a 10-foot emanation of psychic energy centered on yourself that lasts for 1 minute or until you become incapacitated. While the emanation is active, once per turn when a creature within it makes a attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can roll 1d6 and subtract the result from that roll. This ability requires no action.

After using this feature, you cannot use it again until you finish a short or long rest, unless you expend one Battle Die to regain its use.

#### Maniacal Influence
At 10th level, your eldritch master’s influence deepens, granting you further power. When a creature affected by your Frenzied Strike’s Frenzied condition is active, that creature regards you and your Cohort as allies. Additionally, when you use your Blitz feature, you may direct any Frenzied creature’s actions, even if it is not normally your ally.

Whenever a Frenzied creature ends its turn, it must make a Wisdom saving throw against your Maneuver save DC. On a failed save, the Frenzied condition persists; on a success, the condition ends.

## Cohort

Your cohort is the most steadfast of allies, is loyal to a fault, and will likely be a lifelong ally and friend. But not all cohorts are cut from the same cloth or possess the same skills—choose from the following non-player characters when you enlist a new cohort.

### Customizing Cohorts
Throughout your adventure, you may wish to
customize your cohort beyond the weapons,
armor, and spells listed in its stat block.

Your
GM decides which equipment your cohort has
proficiency with, in addition to those specified
in the Cohort feature, and decides if your cohort
can exchange the spells it knows with the others
available to a class, including new spells in this
book.

As with any character, your cohort can attune to three magic items at a time.



### Cohort Spells
Throughout your adventure, you may wish to
customize the spells listed in a Cohort’s stat block.
You can exchange one of the spells listed in the
Spellcasting feature with another of the same level.
Your GM decides if a spell is appropriate for a Cohort.
Generally, the Priest’s spells are from the Cleric spell
list, the Mage’s spells are from the Wizard spell list,
and the Shaman’s spells are from the Druid spell list.

\column

### Recruiting a New Cohort
When a cohort dies, replacing them isn’t
as simple as summoning a new familiar or
placing a sack of gold into the hands of a
hireling. You must first earn the trust of
a potential cohort before initiating them
into your service. This might require a
lengthy quest, the gift of a magic item, or
just a few nights of drinking at the local
tavern. You can also use the following
hooks to introduce a new cohort:

**Cousin, Sibling, or Twin.** Just after the
death of your cohort, a near-identical
relative sweeps into town asking about
them. If you can convince them to
join your cause, you’ll have a seamless
replacement.

**Mysterious Benefactor.** Someone with
deep pockets wants to see your quest
completed and sends a skilled mercenary
to join you following your cohort’s death.
This mercenary pledges their services, but
do they have ulterior motives?

**Reanimation.** Your cohort was an
irreplaceable friend and confidant. Instead
of looking for a new ally to follow your
banner, you enlisted the services of a
necromancer to reanimate your cohort as
an undead. They’re as loyal as always, but
you can’t quite get past the smell.

### Cohort Species
When you initiate a new Humanoid Cohort, you can also give it one of the following traits to reflect its species.

**Dragonborn:**:: *Draconic Ancestry.* The Cohort has Resistance to one of the following types of damage associated with its dragon progenitor (Captain’s choice): Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, or Poison.
**Dwarf:**:: *Resistances and Immunities.* The Cohort has Resistance to Poison damage and Immunity to the Poisoned condition.
**Elf:**:: *Fey Ancestry.* The Cohort has Advantage on saving throws it makes to avoid or end the Charmed condition.
**Gnome:**:: *Saving Throws.* The Cohort has proficiency in one of the following saving throws: Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma.
**Halfling:**:: *Brave.* The Cohort has Advantage on saving throws it makes to avoid or end the Frightened condition.
**Human:**:: *Skillful.* The Cohort has proficiency with one skill of your choice.
**Orc:**:: *Relentless (1/Day).* When the Cohort is reduced to 0 Hit Points, it is reduced to 1 Hit Point instead.
**Tiefling:**:: *Fiendish Ancestry.* The Cohort has Resistance to one of the following types of damage associated with its fiendish progenitor (Captain’s choice): Fire, Necrotic, or Poison.

#### Stalker
Stealthy and subversive, the Stalker is just as adept an assassin as a burglar or thief.


{{monster,frame  
## Stalker
*Medium Humanoid (Any Race), Any Aligment*
___
**Armor Class** :: 14 (studded leather armor)
**Hit Points**  :: 6 plus six times your Captain level (the stalker has a number of Hit Dice [d8s] equal to your Captain level)
**Speed**       :: 30ft.
___
|  STR  |  DEX  |  CON  |  INT  |  WIS  |  CHA  |
|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|
|9 (−1) |14 (+2)|11 (+0)|13 (+1)|12 (+1)|11 (+0)|
___
**Skills** :: Acrobatics +4, Stealth +4
**Saving Throws** :: Dex +4, Int +3
**Condition Immunities** :: None
**Senses**               :: Passive Perception 11
**Proficiencies**:: Simple weapons, Martial weapons that have the Finesse or Light property; Light armor
**Gear**:: Shortbow, 2 Shortswords, Studded Leather Armor
**Languages** :: Common plus one other language
**CR** :: None (XP 0; PB equals your Proficiency Bonus)
___

***Sneak Attack (1/Turn).*** The stalker deals an extra 3 (1d6) damage when it hits a target with a weapon attack and has advantage on the attack roll, or when the target is within 5 feet of an ally of the stalker that isn't incapacitated and the stalker doesn't have disadvantage on the attack roll.

### Actions
***Shortsword.*** *Melee Weapon Attack:* Bonus equals your Charisma modifier + your Proficiency Bonus, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 1d6 + Bonus equals your Charisma modifier piercing damage.
:
***Shortbow.*** *Ranged Weapon Attack:* Bonus equals your Charisma modifier + your Proficiency Bonus, reach 80/200 ft., one target. *Hit:* 1d6 + Bonus equals your Charisma modifier piercing damage.
}}

{{monster,frame 
## Stalker - Cohort
While following the command of a captain, the stalker gains additional abilities based on the captain’s level:

2nd. **Cunning Action.** On each of its turns, the stalker can use a bonus action to take the Dash, Disengage, or Hide action.

6th. **Sneak Attack.** The stalker’s Sneak Attack damage increases to 2d6.

10th. **Uncanny Dodge.** When the stalker takes damage from an effect it can see, it can halve that damage as a reaction.

10th. **Sneak Attack.** The stalker’s Sneak Attack damage increases to 3d6.

13th. **Evasion.** If the stalker is subjected to an effect that allows it to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, the stalker instead takes no damage if it succeeds on the saving throw, and only half damage if it fails.

13th. **Sneak Attack.** The stalker’s Sneak Attack
damage increases to 4d6.

17th. **Assassinate.** During its first turn, the stalker has advantage on attack rolls against any creature that hasn't taken a turn. Any hit the stalker scores against a surprised creature is a critical hit.

#### Survivor
Survivors are not heroes of legend or knights in shining armor; they are hard-bitten realists who have endured the worst the world can throw at them and lived to tell the tale. They fight not for glory, but for the only thing that matters: the next sunrise.


{{monster,frame  
## Survivor
*Medium Humanoid (Any Race), Any Aligment*
___
**Armor Class** :: 14 (Studded leather armor)
**Hit Points**  :: 7 plus six times your Captain level (the survivor has a number of Hit Dice [d8s] equal to your Captain level)
**Speed**       :: 30ft.
___
|  STR  |  DEX  |  CON  |  INT  |  WIS  |  CHA  |
|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|
|14 (+2)|14 (+2)|13 (+1)|10 (+0)|12 (+1)| 9 (-1)|
___
**Skills** :: Athletics +4, Survival +3
**Saving Throws** :: Str +4, Con +3
**Proficiencies**:: Simple and Martial weapons; Light, Shields
**Gear**:: Studded leather armor, Longsword, Shortbow
**Senses**:: Passive Perception 11
**Languages**:: Common plus one other language
**CR**::None (XP 0; PB equals your Proficiency Bonus)
___

### Actions
***Longsword.*** *Melee Attack Roll:* Bonus equals your Charisma modifier plus your Proficiency Bonus, reach 5 ft. *Hit:* 1d10 plus your Charisma modifier Slashing damage.
:
****Shortbow.**** *Ranged Attack Roll:* Bonus equals your Charisma modifier plus your Proficiency Bonus, range 60/120 ft. *Hit:* 1d6 plus your Charisma modifier Piercing damage.

}}



{{monster,frame 
## Survivor - Cohort
While following the command of a captain, the survivor gains additional abilities based on the captain’s level:

2nd. **Wanderer's Secret.** The cohort chooses one of the following secrets:

- ***Avenger.*** The cohort gains a bonus to its Initiative rolls equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum +1).
- ***Fugitive.*** The cohort's speed increases by 10 feet.
- ***Monstrous:.*** The cohort gains Darkvision 60 ft.

6th. **Ambush Reflexes.** The survivor can't be surprised while it is conscious.

6th: **Breather.** As a bonus action, the survivor can expend one of its Hit Dice to regain hit points.

10th. **Desperate Fury.** While the survivor is Bloodied, its weapon attacks deal an extra 1d8 damage on a hit.

13th. **Desperate Survival.** While the survivor is Bloodied, critical hits against it become normal hits.

13th. **Last Stand (1/Long Rest).** When the survivor is reduced to 0 Hit Points and not killed outright, it can choose to drop to 1 Hit Point instead.

17th. **Wayworn.** The survivor is always considered Bloodied for the purpose of its features.

}}

#### Templar
A daring knight, sworn to virtue and station, a
templar knows no fear in their heart and no equal in
battle.


{{monster,frame

*Medium Humanoid (Any Race), Any Aligment*

**Armor Class** :: 16 (chain mail)
**Hit Points**  :: 6 plus six times your Captain level (the templar has a number of Hit Dice [d8s] equal to your Captain level)

## Templar - Cohort
While following the command of a captain, the
templar gains additional abilities based on the
captain’s level:

2nd. **Divine Hands (1/Long).** As an action, the
templar can touch a willing creature within 5 feet of it, which regains 2d8 hit points.

6th. **Spellcasting.** The templar is a 2nd-level
spellcaster. Its spellcasting ability is Charisma
(spell save DC equals your Cohort save DC).
The templar has the following paladin spells
prepared:

1st level (3 slots): *command, divine favor, protection from evil and good*

6th: **Divine Eminence.** As a bonus action, the templar can expend a spell slot to cause its melee weapon attacks to magically deal an extra 10 (3d6) radiant damage to a target on a hit. This benefit lasts until the end of the turn. If the templar expends a spell slot of 2nd level or higher, the extra damage increases by 1d6 for each level above 1st.

10th. **Spellcasting.** The templar’s spellcasting
improves. It is a 3rd-level spellcaster which has
the following paladin spells prepared:

1st level (4 slots): *command, divine favor, protection from evil and good*

2nd level (2 slots): *branding smite*

13th. **Parry.** As a reaction, the templar adds 2 to
its AC against one melee attack that would hit it.
To do so, the templar must see the attacker and
be wielding a melee weapon.

13th. **Spellcasting.** The templar’s spellcasting improves. It is a 4th-level spellcaster which has
the following paladin spells prepared:

1st level (4 slots): *command, divine favor, protection from evil and good*

2nd level (3 slots): *branding smite, magic weapon*

17th. **Spellcasting.** The templar’s spellcasting improves. It is a 5th-level spellcaster which has the following paladin spells prepared:

1st level (4 slots): *command, divine favor, protection from evil and good*

2nd level (3 slots): *branding smite, magic weapon*

3rd level (2 slots): *spirit guardians* (counts as a paladin spell for the templar)
